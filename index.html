About

welcome to Maplesh Fitness and Health

Maplesh is your gym and nutrition partner.

Physical activity or exercise can improve your health and reduce the risk of developing several diseases like type 2 diabetes, cancer and cardiovascular disease.

We're here to help you focus on these Top 3 aspects


  • NUTRITION: Nutrition is one of the most important components of a lifestyle change, but it’s often overlooked. People assume that if they’re working out and sleeping well, they can eat whatever they want without consequence; that belief is far from true.
  • EXERCISE: Believe it or not, your body loves to exercise. It loves to stretch its muscles and loosen its joints, to purge the old toxins and release naturally uplifting chemicals. Your body loves to exercise, even if you’ve convinced yourself that you don’t.
  • ACCOUNTABILITY: A lack of accountability is perhaps the most common obstacle for individuals who want to make big lifestyle changes. Without a strong sense of weight loss accountability, your new exercise routine and nutritional regimen will surely diminish over time.

We create health programs that are customized to each individual employee’s needs. Each program includes a personalized nutrition and exercise plan. We provide the tools, resources, and education to help them change their habits, behavior, and lifestyles successfully.

Sport

Playing sports helps strengthen bones. Sports help aid coordination, balance and flexibility. Many sports can help improves stamina and concentration. Sports allow you to experience the highs and lows of both winning and losing!

Exercise

Exercise can help prevent excess weight gain or help maintain weight loss. When you engage in physical activity, you burn calories. The more intense the activity, the more calories you burn.

Gym Coach

Gym Coach is like having a Personal workout trainer and fitness trainer in your pocket. You’ll quickly improve fitness, strength, tone, muscle and overall body and mental fitness.

Outdoor Activities

The human brain is wired to be outside, and though scientific research supports the notion that being in nature is good for our psychological health, we don’t need the data to know that being outside feels great.
